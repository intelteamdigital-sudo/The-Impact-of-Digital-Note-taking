<h2 style="text-align: center; font-family: times new roman; font-size: 40px">INTRODUCTION</h2><p> </p>
<p style="text-indent: 50px; font-family:times new roman; line-height: 2.0; font-size: 20px">   
       Existing research on digital note-taking offers a range of perspectives on its role in learning. Abad and Gorospe (2023) have identified key advantages, including improved organization of materials, easy access across devices, enhanced efficiency in editing content, and better security for preserving work over time. Additionally, Mendoza (2024) studies how multimedia features allow students to combine text, images, and audio, making it easy for them to process information. 

</p><p style="text-indent: 50px; font-family:times new roman; line-height: 2.0; font-size: 20px" "="">   
          However, despite the growing use of these tools in schools, their overall impact remains poorly defined and inconsistent. While some research suggests digital note-taking boosts learning efficiency (Dizon, 2023), other studies question whether it truly enhances academic performance compared to traditional paper-based methods (Lopez and Reyes, 2022). There is limited research focused specifically on junior high school populations in local institutions like EASC, leaving a significant gap in understanding how these tools affect learners at this critical development stage (Fernandez, 2023). 



</p><p style="text-indent: 50px; font-family:times new roman; line-height: 2.0; font-size: 20px">   
            This study seeks to address these gaps by examining how mobile phone-based digital note-taking influences EASC junior high school students’ academic performance, while also assessing its effects on cognitive thinking processes such as information retention, critical analysis, and knowledge organization. The findings will provide specific insights that can guide teachers, administrators, and parents in supporting students to use digital tools effectively (Aquino et al., 2024). By clarifying both the benefits and challenges of this practice, the research will contribute to more informed decisions about adopting technology into junior high school education at Estrella Academy and to the other school.  



</p><p style="text-indent: 50px; font-family:times new roman; line-height: 2.0; font-size: 20px">   
           Republic Act No. 10533, also known as the Enhanced Basic Education Act of 2013, highlights the importance of integrating technology in the curriculum to enhance learning. This law allows students to innovatively use technology for learning purposes (Congress of the Philippines, 2013). Furthermore, Department of Education (DepEd) Order No. 21, s. 2019 or Policy Guidelines on the Use of ICT in Education, promotes the integration of Information and Computer Technology in classrooms indicates that the use of digital note-taking on mobile devices is an example of ICT (Department of Education, 2019). Lastly, Republic Act No. 10929 or Free Internet Access in Public Places Act ensures that students can access online educational resources where digital note-taking tools rely on internet connectivity for cloud storage, collaborative features, and multimedia integration (Congress of the Philippines, 2017).

<h2 style="text-align: center; font-family: times new roman; font-size: 40px">BACKGROUND OF THE STUDY</h2><p> </p>
<p style="text-indent: 50px; font-family:times new roman; line-height: 2.0; font-size: 20px">   
Digital note-taking refers to the use of technology in recording, organizing, and managing notes. In today’s society, technology plays an important role in different aspects of life, including social, personal, and academic activities. In education, digital note-taking allows students to review, search, and organize their notes more efficiently. According to Abad and Gorospe (2023), digital note-taking helps improve students’critical thinking skills as it encourages them to process in different ways.

</p><p style="text-indent: 50px; font-family:times new roman; line-height: 2.0; font-size: 20px">Learning experiences are the activities and interactions through which learners gain knowledge, skills, and mindsets. They are important for developing students’ comprehension and advancing their academic growth. Favorable learning experiences have a considerable influence on students’academic performance. Martin and Bolliger (2020) state that involving learning experiences boosts students’ engagement and grasp of material, especially when teaching. This indicates that active involvement in learning leads to more effective knowledge. 
</p><p style="text-indent: 50px; font-family:times new roman; line-height: 2.0; font-size: 20px">    Digital note-taking has become increasingly accessible and efficient for teenagers. Alexander et al. (2024) found that most teenagers use their mobile phones for approximately five hours daily, making digital tools a familiar part of their lives. Since the introduction of technology in the 1980s, digital devices and applications for note-taking have continuously evolved. These advancements have made digital note-taking more effective in encoding, organizing, and recalling information, including the ability to record conversation in verbatim. 

</p><p style="text-indent: 50px; font-family:times new roman; line-height: 2.0; font-size: 20px">  At present, many schools and universities have implemented digital note-taking in their learning systems. Studies conducted in these institutions recommend the use of digital note-taking due to its positive effects on learning academic performance (Cortez et al., 2023) (Abad &amp; Gorospe, 2023).  Nowadays, when teachers open the slides, students take out their computers and type notes as the teacher speaks. This shows that since the pandemic, students rely more on technology for note-taking, a trend that continues today (Deshpande et al., 2022). In Estrella Academy, Junior High School students currently use Quipper, which serves as a form of digital learning during classes. 

</p><p style="text-indent: 50px; font-family:times new roman; line-height: 2.0; font-size: 20px">  Furthermore, technology has become a significant component of contemporary learning experiences. Bond et al. (2023) explain that digital learning platforms enhance educational experiences by offering interactive materials and suitable access to learning tools. Collaborative platforms enable students to work together on projects regardless of location, promoting critical thinking and communication skills that contribute to better academic outcomes (Rodriguez et al., 2023). 

</p><p style="text-indent: 50px; font-family:times new roman; line-height: 2.0; font-size: 20px">  In conclusion, this study aims to understand the significance and influence of digital note-taking on learning experiences and the academic performance of Junior High School students at Estrella Academy. The results of this study may help the school and future students adopt the effective use of available digital technologies in learning. The researchers also want to contribute to the country by unlocking the potential of digital note-taking for the education system. By this, we can expand the knowledge of the students and improve the curriculum.

<p style="text-indent: 50px; font-family:times new roman; line-height: 2.0; font-size: 20px"> Digital note-taking is increasingly being adopted by junior high school students at Estrella Academy, with its implementation connecting with multiple key areas we've studied. While digital tools offer potential advantages for learning, there remains a limited understanding of how this practice specifically affects Estrella Academy Junior High students, particularly in relation to issues like a lack of digital knowledge and limited digital resources.
